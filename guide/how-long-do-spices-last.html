How Long Do Spices Last?

Whole spices, ground spices, and blends all have different shelf lives — here's a practical breakdown.

As a general rule, whole spices last the longest — around 3-4 years — because their essential oils are sealed inside the seed or pod until it's cracked or ground. Ground spices lose potency faster, typically staying at their best for about 2-3 years.

Blended masalas, like garam masala or chai masala, tend to have a slightly shorter window — around 1-2 years — since they combine multiple ground spices, each fading at its own rate.

None of these are hard safety limits; properly stored spices rarely become unsafe to eat, they just gradually lose aroma and flavour intensity. The real test is your nose: if a spice still smells strong and characteristic, it's still doing its job.
